Cooked to Catastrophe

Description: Cooked to Catastrophe was developed as the main project for a course focused on Game Development Processes. It is meant to be an educational game that can teach its players various cooking techniques through gameplay as well as teaching recipes and cooking jargon through an in-game cookbook. The game itself is set in a post-apocalyptic world where the culinary arts have been lost. While playing, players are expected to take orders from guests, gather ingredients from an Infinite Pantry, and cook the dishes that were ordered.
In addition to developing the actual game, the development process for this project included creating a comprehensive design document and thoroughly documenting all work. Each team was also meant to keep careful record of the tasks that were being accomplished through the use of task management software and scrum practices.
The game does have its limitations in its current iteration as the development time was limited due to the requirements dictated by the class for the design document. As a result, the mechanics for most recipes are in place, but only 3 recipes are fully implemented. Still, it serves as a fairly successful proof of concept.

Role: Project Lead - I worked on Cooked to Catastrophe as the Project Lead, setting up meetings, organizing the structure of the project, and keeping other members of the team on task. However, I also wrote a significant part of the code for the UI design and interactions.

Language(s) Used: C#

Tool(s) Used: Unity, Visual Studio, Jira
