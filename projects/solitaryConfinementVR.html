Solitary Confinement VR

Description: I had the opportunity to work on this project through the internship I completed at Georgia State University through their NSF-funded Research Experience for Undergraduates program in Immersive Media Computing.
The experience was created as a part of a professor's research into the difference between the effects of a VR experience, a 360 video, a regular video, and pictures. As a result, it is designed to replicate an actual solitary confinement cell as closely as possible. I even had the opportunity to communicate with former inmates and have them test the experience.
In addition to the former inmates, I also had the opportunity to communicate with other faculty at Georgia State to create the most realistic auditory experience, with respect to the space and environment, as well as the most realistic narrative for the experience.

Role: Sole Designer - I worked on this project essentially as the sole designer, creating and placing the 3D models, editing and placing the audio, etc. Though, I did have a supervisor I had to check-in with throughout the summer.

Language(s) Used: C#

Tool(s) Used: Unity, SteamVR, Visual Studio, HTC Vive
